Egyptian Astronomy
Teaching
 

 

The Egyptians 6000 years ago, watched the skies not for the fun of it, but to observe, measure and take down the position of the stars throughout the year to correlate them with seasons, climate, floods and other natural occurrences. Their drive was not science or curiosity, but agriculture! 

Chinese Inventories
Work
 

The Chinese had a log for almost anything that happened on their lands. They recorded of course every foreign visit, they inventoried every royal gift to envoys and merchants. And because they collected those sets of data and saved them for the future, we now know that The Travels of Marco Polo is the work of a 13th century romantic author Rustichello of Pisa. We know that Marco Polo is a fictional character, simply because if he actually lived and visited China he would be on the record. 

1798 Malthusian Theory
Teaching
 

In his 1798 book An Essay on the Principle of Population, Malthus observed that an increase in a nation's food production improved the well-being of the populace, but the improvement was temporary because it led to population growth, which in turn restored the original per capita production level. In other words, humans had a propensity to utilize abundance for population growth rather than for maintaining a high standard of living, a view that has become known as the "Malthusian trap" or the "Malthusian spectre". Populations had a tendency to grow until the lower class suffered hardship, want and greater susceptibility to famine and disease, a view that is sometimes referred to as a Malthusian catastrophe.

 

1990 S.E.T.I.
Teaching
 

Space! The universe, the galaxies, the planetary systems are as real as your ordinary apple on the kitchen table. The difference is that because of the enormous scale of the universe and the gravely limited human sensory systems, space can only be conceived through the collection of observational data, wavelength recordings, correlations, maps, imaging. In other words if it were not for data visualization we could not possibly “see” the constellation Coma Berenices, not even our giant neighbor Jupiter!

 

If “seeing” is such a problem in our universe, imagine “hearing”! The Search For Extraterrestrial Intelligence at Berkeley SETI Research Center turned to the public for help in their quest to scan the vastness of space for transmissions that could reveal intelligent communication. In 1999, under the flag of SETI@home, scientists invited hundreds of thousands volunteers willing to offer idle computer power to process the zillions of data recorded from the central 2.5 MHz wide band of the SERENDIP IV receiver. 290,000 computers give SETI@home an average computational power of 617 teraFLOPS. What a collective effort for what most of us could pass as (space) oddity!

 

1999 Information Visualization: Perception for Design
Award
 

Data collection, processing, evaluation and visualisation have been closely related to the successful development of agriculture, astronomy, climatology, health and healing, warfare, transport, industrial production, security, politics. Working with data could lead anyone to interesting discoveries, new ideas and challenges of old perceptions.  

 

Colin Ware’s “Information visualization, perception for design” helped me get a better understanding of how the new “cognitive tools” and the creative mind lead us to the improvement of sciences, engineering, banking, business, and the arts. A designer is now capable to search for data, select them, make correlations, create algorithms and present their results using design (digits, graphs, informatics/poster, video, publication, website…etc) hoping to make people question their beliefs and think again.

2000 Y2K bug
Teaching
 

The Y2K bug was a computer flaw, or bug, that may have caused problems when dealing with dates beyond December 31, 1999. The flaw, faced by computer programmers and users all over the world on January 1, 2000, is also known as the "millennium bug." (The letter K, which stands for kilo (a unit of 1000), is commonly used to represent the number 1,000. So, Y2K stands for Year 2000.) Many skeptics believe it was barely a problem at all.

2019 Now
Education
 

My role as a designer should be to effectively communicate whatever needs to be communicated. Even more so, when I come across something worth fighting for. I then need to convince others to join in but instead of just raising the alarm about a present danger, I prefer to suggest a solution to a problem. In my view, nothing positive comes out of anger, fear or depression. Fearing or fighting the use of data will never get us to a better life and the same is true for any over-simplification of social problems. 

 

Bruno Latour states that when we create an image we generate the sense that what this image depicts is real. This is why data visualization can be a very dangerous or a very useful visual tool of persuasion and propaganda. Other possible data related risks are privacy and human rights violations. What I find most alarming though, is the the energy consumption of data storing and processing, because it directly endangers the natural environment. 

 

In 2017 we generated more data than in the previous 5,000 years of human existence. Most of the energy is consumed for the cooling of the data servers, the data centers which are vast physical support infrastructures, huge buildings, electrical equipment and air-conditioning mechanical plants. Facebook recently opened a new data center at Luella in Northern Sweden to use the Arctic air and cool its servers. Microsoft plans to place its data centers on the ocean floor of the coast of California to take advantage of the low water temperatures. The problem is growing, however, and we need to address it.  As a team of students, we came up with something involving a collective effort, a solution with a strong social character.  We wanted a positive answer instead of just a  denial. But first let’s appreciate the data for the culture they evolved today. 

The Future
Education
 

As soon as our team was introduced to the “data design” assignment, we started researching relevant sources. After reading an article about how Data Centres Of The World Will Consume 1/5 Of Earth’s Power By 2025, it seemed to me that this is a very contemporary and alarming issue that needs to be dealt with. We also found that big companies such as Google or Amazon use up an extremely big amount of electricity to power their Data Centers, where they store all the material drawn from their websites. From Google's recently published paper "Achieving Our 100% Renewable Energy Purchasing Goal and Going Beyond", it is stated that the company's data centers consume approximately 5.7 Terawatt hours annually, which is comparable to the whole city of San Francisco's annual electricity consumption. 

 

By analyzing those sets of information we came up with the idea to create an algorithm that enables you to see how much energy your data use simply by interacting online. When uploading, downloading, transferring, or storing data, energy is consumed in data centers, servers and computers, which has an important environmental impact. With a simple rule of three we converted the values to Kilo Bytes and Watts and we presented this research on a webpage.

 

This project showed to me that data visualization can be a very effective method of persuasion. According to Ware “Visualization has now come to mean something more like a graphical representation of data or concepts. Thus, from being an internal construct of the mind, a visualization has become an external artifact supporting decision making”.  He becomes more clear with reference to Saussure’s perception of language as a system of sings, where a sign (an image or a written word) consists of a signifier and a signified, the relation of which communicates a meaning and Barthes’ denotation, the descriptive level of a sign linked to broader cultural themes, concepts and meanings. 

 

Looking back on our website and the algorithm there, I can see it as promotional tool for our vision and the future of our project -a communication campaign that will introduce the context and inform people about the first human-powered energy cloud. This new eco-friendly cloud can become our brand and it will be based on a new algorithm that enables you to provide yourself sufficient power for the files you store online, with the use  of a simple dynamo attached to your computer or any other possible source of energy.
